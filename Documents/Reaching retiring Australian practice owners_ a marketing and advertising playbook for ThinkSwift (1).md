# **Reaching retiring Australian practice owners: a marketing and advertising playbook for ThinkSwift**

**The opportunity is enormous and the competitive landscape is wide open.** Roughly 400,000 Australian small businesses need new ownership within the next decade, driven by the baby boomer retirement wave — yet only 24% of SME owners have any succession plan at all. For professional services firms specifically, 68% of practice owners have no formal succession plan, and the niche intersection of AI-powered business systemisation and exit-readiness preparation is completely uncontested. ThinkSwift can claim a genuine whitespace position as the firm that makes professional services practices operationally sellable. This report covers keyword data, search trends, channel analysis, cost benchmarks, competitive intelligence, ROI modelling, and partnership strategy for a $2,000–$5,000/month marketing budget.

## **A demographic tidal wave is driving search volumes upward**

Every data point confirms that searches related to business exit and succession planning are increasing in Australia, powered by a structural demographic shift that will accelerate through at least 2030\.

**The numbers are staggering.** PwC analysis estimates **1.4 million private business owners** employing 7.9 million people and contributing approximately $500 billion in GDP are expected to retire within the next decade. The MYOB Bi-Annual Business Monitor (2024) found that **48% of baby boomer SME owners plan to exit within one to five years**, yet only **24% have any succession plan**. The Exit Planning Institute reports that focus on exit planning has increased **fivefold since 2013**. Family Business Australia estimates a wealth transfer of **$3.5 trillion** as 81% of family business owners intend to retire within ten years.

For professional services specifically, the situation is even more acute. CPA Australia now **mandates documented succession plans** under APES 325 Risk Management for Firms. DMY, Australia's leading accounting practice broker, reports record-high listings with **75+ practices sold in just three years** and 72 active buyers per metro listing. Accounting practice valuations typically range from **0.8x to 2.0x annual revenue**, with firms that have proper succession planning and documented systems commanding **20–30% higher sale multiples** — a fact that directly validates ThinkSwift's systemisation value proposition.

Seasonal patterns align with the Australian financial year: expect search spikes in **April–June** (pre-EOFY, owners assessing their position), **July–September** (post-EOFY, owners reviewing annual results and making strategic decisions), and **January–February** (New Year resolution-driven planning). ABS data confirms strong seasonal patterns in business exits clustering around financial year boundaries.

## **Which advertising channels actually reach 55-to-70-year-old practice owners**

Not all channels are created equal for this demographic. The target — professional services firm owners aged 55–70 in Australia — is a small, identifiable, high-value audience that requires precision over volume.

**LinkedIn is the precision instrument.** Australia has **17 million LinkedIn users** (61.7% of the population), with 9.2% being baby boomers aged 61–79 and 27.2% being Gen X aged 45–60. The ability to target by job title (Partner, Principal, Director, Owner), industry (Accounting, Legal, Financial Services), seniority level, company size (1–50 employees), and years of experience (20+) makes LinkedIn unmatched for B2B professional services targeting. LinkedIn B2B leads convert at **2.74%** — nearly three times higher than Facebook or Twitter. Sponsored Content, Message Ads (InMail), and Document Ads (sharing gated guides) are the most effective formats for this audience. Thought Leader Ads — boosting organic posts from personal profiles — deliver **3x higher CTR** than standard company ads at 40% lower cost per lead.

**Google Search Ads capture active intent.** When someone searches "sell my accounting practice," they are genuinely considering selling. Google Search is essential for capturing this bottom-of-funnel demand, even though total search volume for niche professional services exit keywords in Australia is relatively modest (a few hundred searches per month across the keyword cluster). The legal and financial services category in Australia sees CPCs ranging from **$5 to $20+** for advisory keywords, with conversion rates of approximately **3–5%** for well-optimised landing pages.

**Facebook offers reach at lower cost.** Facebook has approximately **4.9 million Australian users aged 55+** (26% of its user base), making it the platform where this demographic spends the most social media time. Targeting options include age, business owner interests, Xero/MYOB users, and retirement planning interests. Facebook is significantly cheaper than LinkedIn (**$2–$4 CPC** versus $6–$12+), but targeting is interest-based rather than verified professional data, so lead quality is lower. Best deployed for retargeting website visitors and distributing content rather than cold prospecting.

**YouTube reaches 83% of Australians aged 50–64** and delivers 2.4x greater lift in purchase intent than other digital platforms according to Kantar Australia. Custom intent audiences based on exit planning search terms can be effective for awareness, but the production investment required makes this a lower priority for a limited budget.

**Industry publications offer direct access to the exact audience.** CPA Australia's INPRACTICE eNewsletter reaches **9,000+ public practice members** — the single most targeted channel available. Key verified advertising rates from CPA Australia's 2025 media kit:

* INPRACTICE monthly newsletter: **$5,000 \+ GST**  
* INTHEBLACK monthly eNewsletter (230,000 subscribers): **$10,000 \+ GST**  
* Sponsored content on INTHEBLACK website: **$10,000 \+ GST**  
* Sponsored webinar: **$15,000 \+ GST**  
* CPA Update fortnightly newsletter (118,000 subscribers): **$5,000 \+ GST**

Accountants Daily, Lawyers Weekly, and Money Management (all Momentum Media brands) offer website ads, sponsored content, webcasts, podcasts, and events — contact their national partnerships manager at 02 8045 2006 for media kits. CA ANZ's Acuity reaches **140,000+ members**. The Law Institute Journal reaches 20,000+ Law Society of Victoria members.

**Direct mail is a sleeper channel for this demographic.** Response rates for direct mail average **4.9% for prospect lists and up to 14.1% for the 45–54 age cohort** — vastly higher than email's \~1%. Among B2B marketers, 75% say direct mail is the best channel to connect with C-suite executives. At approximately **$3–$8 per piece** including design, printing, and postage in Australia, a targeted campaign of 100–200 personalised letters per month to accounting firm principals identified through CPA Australia's "Find a CPA" directory or ASIC company searches could generate meaningful leads.

**Speaking engagements deliver the highest ROI of any B2B channel** — First Page Sage data from 150+ projects shows **856% ROI** with $518 customer acquisition cost. CPA Australia hosts 1,600+ events annually, FAAA has 30 local chapters with regular events, and state law societies run ongoing CPD sessions. Many local chapter events accept external speakers at no cost.

## **How the competition markets their exit planning services**

The Australian exit planning market is fragmented across three tiers — advisory firms, specialist practice brokers, and general business brokers — and **no competitor occupies ThinkSwift's proposed position** at the intersection of AI-powered systemisation and exit readiness for professional services.

**Succession Plus** is the content marketing leader in the space. Founded by Dr Craig West, they operate a sophisticated HubSpot-powered funnel featuring a "Content Hub" blog, a 21-day audio series called "Exit Like a Boss," free book offers, gated webinars, and endorsements from Mark Bouris and Michael Pascoe. Their primary differentiator is Employee Share Ownership Plans (ESOPs). They target general SME business owners — not professional services specifically — and focus on financial and structural planning, not operational preparation.

**Exit Advisory Group** runs the "Buy Grow Sell Podcast," publishes on Forbes (CEO Simon Bedard is NSW Chair of the Australian Institute of Business Brokers), offers a gated "10-Step Checklist to Sell Your Business," and is active on LinkedIn with educational content. They claim **$500M+ in managed transactions** and focus on lower-middle market businesses broadly.

**DMY** is the most relevant niche competitor — Australia's leading specialist M\&A advisor for accounting practices, operating the Practice4Sale platform. They've sold **75+ practices in three years**, serve only sellers (not buyers), and publish compelling market data showing metro practices averaging **$1.14 per $1 of fees** with 72 buyers per listing and 80% selling within 90 days. Their marketing is data-driven and highly credible but transaction-focused — they help sell, not prepare.

**Rob Knights & Co** has 50+ years of experience with Australian accounting and law firms, offering both succession planning and practice broking. They publish blog content on the future of accounting and AI impacts. **Practice Succession (Colin Simkin)** is another specialist broker for accounting and law practices with active listings from $330K to $4.48M in fees.

Other notable players include **The Exit Strategy Group** (Melbourne-based, founded by Kerry Boulton, focused on general SME exit strategy), **Xcllusive Business Sales** (national brokerage with a bold "120-day buyer guarantee"), **Benchmark Business Sales** (national brokerage with the "Meaning Business Podcast"), and **William Buck** (major accounting firm publishing the annual "Exit Smart Report"). Marketplace platforms like **Bsale** (697+ listed brokers) and **SEEK Business** serve as listing channels.

### **The competitive gaps ThinkSwift should exploit**

Seven clear whitespace opportunities emerged from the competitive analysis:

* **No firm combines operational systemisation with exit preparation.** Every competitor focuses on financial planning or deal execution. Nobody helps owners build the transferable operational systems that actually make a business sellable and valuable.  
* **No AI-native player exists in the exit planning space.** Knowledge capture, process documentation, and workflow automation using AI tools is a completely unclaimed position.  
* **Law firm succession is massively underserved.** Accounting practice broking is well-established, but there is no dedicated Australian law firm succession specialist. The UK and US have specialist legal practice brokers; Australia has a void.  
* **"Business systemisation" as an exit strategy has no champion.** The phrase barely appears in competitor marketing, yet industry research consistently identifies documented processes and reduced key-person risk as the primary value drivers for practice sales.  
* **The $2M–$10M professional services firm is ignored.** Big 4 firms serve enterprise. Brokers serve when it's time to transact. Nobody helps the 10–50 employee practice prepare two to five years ahead of exit.  
* **Content marketing across the sector is generally weak.** Apart from Succession Plus's sophisticated content engine, most competitors have minimal or inconsistent content strategies, leaving room for a content-dominant entrant.

## **Referral partnerships are the most valuable long-term channel**

The path to a retiring practice owner almost always runs through their trusted advisors — their accountant, financial planner, or business broker. Building referral partnerships is the lowest-cost, highest-quality lead generation channel available, though it takes three to six months to mature.

**Accountants are the first point of contact for nearly every business exit.** MYOB's CEO has stated that "a great place to start is a conversation with a trusted advisor, such as your accountant." Under CPA Australia's APES 325, all public practice firms must now document succession plans — creating a compliance trigger that ThinkSwift can help address. The average age of public practitioners is well over 50, meaning many accountants are simultaneously advising clients on exit and facing their own succession challenges. ThinkSwift should offer an "Exit Readiness Scorecard" that accountants can use with their clients, positioning ThinkSwift as a value-add to the accountant's existing advisory relationship.

**Business brokers are the most direct referral source.** Multiple brokers explicitly recommend pre-sale preparation — SBA Brokers advises clients to "document key procedures" and notes that "the more turnkey your business appears, the more confidence buyers will have." This is precisely ThinkSwift's service. The optimal partnership structure positions ThinkSwift **before the broker in the value chain**: brokers refer clients who aren't "sale-ready" to ThinkSwift for systemisation, and ThinkSwift refers "sale-ready" clients back to brokers for listing. Since ThinkSwift's work increases sale price (and therefore broker commission), this creates aligned incentives.

The most important firms to approach for partnership include **DMY** (Practice4Sale — they only broker accounting practices and would benefit from a preparation partner), **Practice Succession (Colin Simkin)** (specialist accounting and law practice broker who runs workshops), **Rob Knights & Co** (50+ years with accounting and law firms), and **Smithink** (consulting advisory to accounting firms, founded by former ICAA president). **FAAA** (Financial Advice Association Australia) operates 30 local chapters with regular events accepting external speakers, providing direct access to financial planners who serve business owner clients.
